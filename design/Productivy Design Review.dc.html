02 · Landing page

A front door, not a features dump

Dropped the fabricated "thousands of users" line — for a portfolio piece, honesty about scope (solo-built, actively used daily) is more persuasive than fake social proof. Tightened the pitch to one sentence, added real credibility signals (stack, source link), and gave feature cards new content that reflects what's actually being added below.

04 · Activity status

From a full-screen chore to something ambient

You said it yourself: you don't bother switching it. That's not a UI polish problem, it's a friction problem — an 8-option modal takeover for something that should be a glance. The fix is two-fold: infer the status automatically whenever a timer is running, and shrink manual switching down to a one-click popover that lives in the header, always visible, never blocking. Down to 6 categories (merged Sleeping + Others into "Off"; Hobbying into "Playing") to cut choice overload.

09 · Timers move into the nav

Pomodoro + Time Worked as nav pills, expanding on click

Both timers sit permanently in the nav as compact pills — a countdown and a progress ring, always visible while you work anywhere in the app. Click either to expand a popover with full controls; click elsewhere to collapse. The project page body is now just the three things it should be: Sprints, Sprint tasks, and the Board — full width, nothing competing with them. The floating button at the bottom is now a 3-way view switcher (All / Sprints & tasks / Board only) instead of a generic "Tools" label — pick the focus that matches what you're doing right now, sprints+tasks on the left and board on the right when split, or one panel full-width when isolated.
